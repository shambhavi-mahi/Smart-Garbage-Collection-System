🗂️ Know Your Waste Categories

Each colour, each category — handled differently to protect our planet.

🔵 DRY WASTE 📅 Mon & Thu
📦

Dry / Recyclable Waste

Items that are dry and can be recycled, repurposed or sold to scrap dealers.

✅ INCLUDE
Paper Cardboard Plastic bottles Cans Glass jars Newspapers Packaging
❌ EXCLUDE
Food-stained paper Wet items Broken glass
🟢 WET WASTE 📅 Daily pickup
🥕

Wet / Organic Waste

Organic material that decomposes quickly and can be composted into rich fertiliser.

✅ INCLUDE
Food scraps Vegetable peels Garden waste Cooked food Tea bags Eggshells
❌ EXCLUDE
Plastic bags Metal Glass
🔴 HAZARDOUS 📅 Monthly drives
☢️

Hazardous Waste

Items that can harm people or the environment — always use designated disposal points.

⚠️ INCLUDES
Batteries Paints Chemicals Medicines Pesticides Motor oil Aerosols
⚠️ NEVER mix with regular waste. Use designated hazardous drop-off points only.
🔵 E-WASTE 📅 Certified centres
💻

E-Waste

Electronic devices contain rare metals and toxic components — always recycle responsibly.

⚠️ INCLUDES
Old phones Laptops Chargers TVs Printers Hard drives Keyboards
📍 Drop off at certified e-waste recycling centres only. Never in regular bins.

💡 Pro Tips for Smarter Sorting

Small habits that make a huge difference for our city.

1
Rinse Before Recycling

Empty and rinse containers before placing in dry waste bins to avoid contamination that ruins entire batches.

2
Compost at Home

A small compost bin cuts truck trips and creates free fertiliser for your garden. Setup takes under 10 minutes.

3
Colour-Code Your Bins

🟢 Green for wet · 🔵 Blue for dry · 🔴 Red for hazardous. Labels prevent costly cross-contamination.

4
Reduce First

The best waste is no waste at all. Choose products with minimal or compostable packaging whenever possible.

5
Report Full Bins Instantly

Use the Resident Portal to report overflowing bins so our AI re-routes trucks immediately — no delays.

6
Use Drop-off Points

Batteries, medicines and electronics have dedicated drop-off kiosks in every colony. Never bin them at home.
