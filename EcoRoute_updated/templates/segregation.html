Waste Segregation Guide

Proper waste segregation is the first step toward a cleaner city. Learn how to sort your waste correctly.

📦

Dry Waste

Items that are dry and can be recycled or repurposed.

✅ INCLUDE:
  • Paper
  • Cardboard
  • Plastic bottles
  • Cans
  • Glass jars
  • Newspapers
  • Packaging
❌ EXCLUDE:
  • Food-stained paper
  • Wet items
  • Broken glass
🗓️ Collection: Monday & Thursday
🥕

Wet Waste

Organic material that can be composted into rich fertiliser.

✅ INCLUDE:
  • Food scraps
  • Vegetable peels
  • Garden waste
  • Cooked food
  • Tea bags
  • Eggshells
❌ EXCLUDE:
  • Plastic
  • Metal
  • Glass
🗓️ Collection: Daily (green bin)
☢️

Hazardous Waste

Items that can harm people or the environment — need special disposal.

⚠️ INCLUDES:
  • Batteries
  • Paints
  • Chemicals
  • Medicines
  • Pesticides
  • Motor oil
  • Aerosols
⚠️ NEVER mix with regular waste. Use designated hazardous waste drop-off points.
🗓️ Collection: Special monthly drives
💻

E-Waste

Electronic devices contain rare metals and toxic components — always recycle.

⚠️ INCLUDES:
  • Old phones
  • Laptops
  • Chargers
  • TVs
  • Printers
  • Hard drives
🗓️ Drop-off: Certified e-waste centres only

💡 Pro Tips

Simple habits that make a big difference.

1
Rinse before recycling — Empty and rinse containers before placing them in dry waste bins to avoid contamination.
2
Compost at home — Start a small compost bin for wet waste. It reduces truck trips and creates fertiliser.
3
Label your bins — Colour-code: 🟢 Green for wet, 🔵 Blue for dry, 🔴 Red for hazardous.
4
Reduce first — The best waste is no waste. Choose products with minimal packaging.
5
Report overflowing bins — Use the Resident Portal to report full bins so trucks are rerouted immediately.
